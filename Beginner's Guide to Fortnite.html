Beginner's Guide to Fortnite

Fortnite Battle Royale has a simple objective. There are 100 players in the Fortnite island, battling until one player/duo/trio/squad is left standing.

Types

There are currently four different Fortnite Battle Royale types: solos, duos, trios and squads. In solos, you go by yourself, in duos, you are with one other player, in trios, you have two teammates and finally, in squads, you have three other teammates.

Fortnite Battle Royale

If you're new to Fortnite, you should probably go with squads in Battle Royale. That means that there are more people to cover for you, more peple to revive you, and more people to reboot you. Of course, Fortnite Battle Royale isn't the only game in fortnite, but more on taht later. The first thing that happens when a game starts is that you start on a flying bus. This bus is called the Battle bus. When the Battle Bus says you can jump off, either jump off immediately or wait a bit so you are closer to your destination. There is a high chance that one or more of your teammates will place a marker in a certain location, and it might be a good idea to go to one of your teammates' marker, because then you know you will be with someone. Once you activate your glider as you approach ground, You can either go to the ground or go on a rooftop. A rooftop might be a good idea, as there are weapons on roofs and if you get in combat with someone on the floor, you have the advantage, as you're on the high ground. Once you've collected the things on the roof, use your harvesting tool to break the chunk of roof under you. Then you'll be inside the house. Listen for the sound of a chest. Chests make a kind of humming noise. If you hear it, you're close to a chest! Try to find out which way the noise is coming from and go that way.

A chest contains gold, weapons, items and ammo. (Example:a small pile of gold, a shockwave grenade, an auto shotgun, auto shotgun ammo.)

There's a chance that you meet someone in the building that you're in. If you do, and it's an enemy, shoot them! Make sure to stock up on med items as well, and shield potions. If you find a chug splash, find a teammate and throw it down between you two! This means that you both will get shield! The chug cannon is even better, but MUCH more hard to obtain, as it's an EXOTIC.

However, the chug cannon takes up two inventory slots instead of one, making it a bit harder to maintain a balance of all types of weapons in your inventory. Just like many other things in Fortnite, the chug cannon can be bought for 600 gold by talking to an NPC. But it can also be obtained from Supply Llamas.

Supply Llamas were added in Chapter 1 Season 3, but at that time they were still a container. They were then upgraded to an animal in Chapter 2 Season 7.

Back to Fortnite...

Once you finish collecting weapons, look at the minimap. The minimap is located at the top right corner if your screen. The white arrow is you. If you see a white line that continues in front of you but disappears behind you (on the map, not the game) then continue following that white line until you reach the inside of a big white circle. (again, on the map, not the game...) The circle is the storm eye. The storm eye is the only place that you are safe from the storm. The storm looks purple on the minimap and the fullscreen map and when you're inside it, it looks purple too. Here's an image of the fullscreen map with the storm eye, the white line and the storm.

If you're in the storm, your health bar starts to drain. Shield doesn't work on it, it only reduces health.. And no, shield can't be used as a subsitute for health. Once you're out of health, you're eliminated. Early on in the game, the storm's damage is small, only 1 damage per second. At it's most damaging point, deep into the game, it does 10 damage per second. The aim of the storm is to group all the players together so they don't get to hide.


Once you get in the storm circle, collect materials. There's (from least to most strong) wood, brick and metal. Wood is obtained from trees and wooden items, etc. Brick is obtained from most walls and rocks, etc. Metal is collected from cars, boats, etc. When you're under attack, BUILD! There are 4 shapes you can build. You can build a ramp/stairs, a wall, a pyramid roof that usually isn't used as a roof and a wall that faces the ground (basically a floor.). TIP: If you're falling and you think you'll take fall damage, build a wall that faces the ground(ok, I'm calling it floor from now on) As fast as you can, then continue your descent to the ground.

And that's how you begin your journey in Fortnite Battle Royale!

Discover

Fortnite isn't just about Battle Royale. If you use the "change" option, you'll be teleported to Discover. There are categories in Discover, such as Recently Played, By Epic, Epic's Picks and tons more. Popular games include Team Rumble, Zero Build and most Deathrun games.

vBucks Store and vBucks

vBucks are Fortnite's in-game currency.

vBucks cannot be obtained for free, no matter what websites tell you, so don't give away any personal information. vBucks can be purchased in the in-game store or at the gaming section at stores like Smyths. vBucks can be spent in the in-game store, which has back blings, skins, gliders, harvesting tools, wraps, emotes,emoticons and sprays. You can also buy the Battle Pass. The Battle Pass has a selection of all the types of things in the in-game item shop. The free Battle Pass still requires leveling up, just like the Battle Pass that you have to buy. The Battle Pass usually costs 950 vBucks, but you have the plus side because the complete Battle Pass usually has 1000-1200 vBucks.
